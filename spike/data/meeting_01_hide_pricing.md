# Client Sync — Acme Corp Website

**Date:** 2026-05-02
**Participants:** Arjun (freelance developer), Nadia (Acme, Head of Growth)

---

**Nadia:** Our enterprise prospects keep hesitating. When they land on the site
and immediately see the number tiers up top, they think we're too small-time for
their scale. The sales team says the bigger accounts want to talk to a human, not
self-serve through a menu of packages.

**Arjun:** So the tier cards up front are actively costing us the larger deals.
Rather than showing everyone a retail-style menu, we should route the serious
buyers straight to a conversation with your team.

**Nadia:** Exactly. The self-serve signposting is fine for small customers, but it
undercuts us with the accounts that actually move the needle on revenue.

**Arjun:** Agreed. I'll rework the top of the landing page so the big buyers get a
direct line to sales instead of a package menu.

---

**Decision:** Prioritise winning larger accounts over self-serve signposting on the
landing hero. Replace the upfront package menu with a direct path to a human
conversation.
